# Little Duck Pond â€” Project Handover

A calming interactive web app: a half-pond / half-grass scene with cute Indian Runner ducks. You tap (or drag) anywhere to drop food pellets; the nearest ducks run or swim over to eat them, then do a little happy celebration. It's meant as a gentle thing to poke at instead of doom-scrolling. It's now an installable PWA deployed on GitHub Pages.

This document is a handover so the project can be continued in a fresh session (e.g. Claude Cowork).

## Current state

- Fully working and deployed to GitHub Pages.
- Installable as a PWA on Android and iPhone, works offline.
- Audio redesigned to a peaceful nature bed (breeze + birdsong) and tuned to taste.
- **A pond now starts with 1 duck and no visitors unlocked** â€” everything else is bought in the shop. Duck count and unlocked visitors are part of the shared (synced) pond record.
- **Live weather + day/night sync** for Wrexham, UK via the Open-Meteo API: real nighttime (deep-blue wash, twinkling stars, glowing moon, dimmed ducks) and rain effects (falling streaks + water-only splash ripples) that mirror the actual local conditions.
- Tighter pellet swarming: up to 4 ducks can chase one pellet, and ducks now re-target every frame instead of locking on.
- **Sleepy idle state**: after a randomised 1-2 minutes without feeding, the flock gathers into a huddle, tucks their bills down, closes their eyes and falls asleep with little floating "z" bubbles â€” a gentle cue to put the phone down. Dropping a pellet wakes them with a quick wing-stretch, then they run for the food.
- **Pond shop**: a lemonade-stand button (under the sound button) opens the shop. Buy a **duck** (costs `500 Ă— current count` â€” 1st extra 500, then +500 each, capped at 100) or unlock a **visitor** type (1,500 Quacks each; once owned it starts dropping by at random). Buying a visitor also summons it immediately so you see your purchase arrive. Everything is paid for in Quacks and shared between both phones (a purchase deducts and appears for both). The visitor rows build automatically from the `VISITORS` registry.
- **Currency ("Quacks")**: a counter (gold-coin pill, top-left under the title) that earns +1 per pellet a duck actually *eats* and +100 per minute the app is open. **Shared between both phones via Firebase Realtime Database** (one `ponds/shared` record; earn/spend are atomic transactions so the two clients can't clobber each other), with `localStorage` as an instant + offline fallback. The display name is the one-line `CURRENCY` constant.
- **Random visitors**: every 1-1.5 minutes (randomised, one at a time) a cute guest wanders in (only from the visitor types you've unlocked in the shop). Built on an extensible visitor framework so more animals can be added. So far:

A **red panda** that ambles calmly around the grass (land only â€” it never enters the water), pausing now and then, for 28-44s before wandering off. It's the friendly opposite of the cat: as it gets near, some of the closest ducks (not all â€” a per-duck roll) toddle over and mill around it in a loose ring, with little hearts popping up; the rest carry on as normal. Driven by a new `befriends` flag and duck `visit` state.

A **family of frogs** â€” one big parent and three babies â€” that hop (proper arcing hops with a squash-and-stretch and a landing splat) around the water's edge in a follow-the-leader line, then hop into the pond with little plops to leave. One curious duck waddles up to investigate the big frog; when it gets close the frog leaps away in fright and the startled duck scurries back the other way for a moment, then plucks up the courage to approach again â€” a cute back-and-forth that repeats until the duck loses interest.

A pair of **dragonflies** that dart erratically over the water (hovering, then zipping to a new spot). One nearby duck may take the bait and chase one â€” but the dragonfly is much faster and simply darts away, so the duck never catches it and loses interest after a few seconds. Only one duck chases at a time.

A **stork** that glides in from the sky, lands at the water's edge, fishes for a bit â€” dipping its long beak down to the water with little ripples each time â€” then takes off and flies away. It has two drawn poses (wings-spread flight vs. tall standing/pecking). While it fishes, nearby ducks are charmed into copying it: one by one they line up along the water's edge beside it and dip their own heads to the water in imitation, all dispersing the moment the stork flies off.

A small **school of 3 koi** that glide calmly around the pond (water only â€” they never cross the shoreline) and pop up to the surface every few seconds with a ripple and a soft "bloop", faint and teal-tinted when deep, bright with a wet back-glint when surfacing. Each fish wanders independently. The ducks completely ignore them (`spooks:false`).

A **ginger cat** that strolls in from the grass, play-stalks and pounces at the ducks (who wake, bunch into a loose huddle and scatter at each pounce, then regroup), meows now and then, and saunters off after 20-30s. When he pounces the flock explodes apart in all directions like startled headless chickens; he then singles out the nearest unlucky duck and chases it around (that duck sprints flat-out and is faster than him, so it stays just ahead), while the rest of the flock â€” now that he's distracted and far away â€” hustle back together. He's a bit slower in water (85% of his land pace) but still quick. The ducks ignore food while he's around.

## How the app is built (architecture)

Everything is inside one IIFE in `index.html`. Rough map, top to bottom:

- **Config / state** â€” globals like `RUN`/`WALK` (duck speeds), `state` (arrays of ducks, pellets, particles, ripples, clouds, lilies, reeds), and the shoreline shape (`shoreSeed`). `shoreX(y)` / `isWater(x,y)` define the wavy boundary between pond (left) and grass (right).
- **`PALS`** â€” array of 4 duck colour palettes (white, fawn-with-neck-ring, cocoa, slate). Each is a set of fill colours for body/belly/wing/head/beak/legs/eye etc.
- **`Duck` class** â€” position, velocity, and a little state machine: `wander â†’ pause/peck â†’ seek â†’ eat â†’ happy â†’ â€¦`. `update(dt)` does the steering/animation; `draw(g)` draws the duck procedurally with canvas paths.
- **Scene building** â€” `renderStatic()` paints the sky, water, grass, shore, grass texture, flowers and pebbles **once** to an offscreen canvas (`bg`) for performance. Lilies, reeds, clouds are positioned in `initLilies/initReeds/initClouds`.
- **Spawns** â€” `dropPellet`, `eatPellet`, and particle spawners (crumbs, hearts, tap rings, ripples).
- **`assignFood()`** â€” each frame, assigns ducks to the best nearby pellet. Targets are re-evaluated every frame (no lock-in), so a duck instantly switches to a closer pellet dropped near it. A small contention penalty plus a per-pellet cap stop more than 4 ducks dogpiling one pellet.
- **Weather sync (`syncWeather()`)** â€” async `fetch` of the Open-Meteo current-conditions API for Wrexham (lat 53.0456, lon -2.9755), reading `is_day` and `weather_code`. WMO codes 51â€“67 and 80â€“82 are treated as rain/drizzle. Results land in the global `weather = {loaded, isDay, rain}`. Called **once at startup**; there's no polling, so state is fixed for the session until reload. Network failures are caught and the app keeps its defaults (day, no rain).
- **Visitors (`manageVisitor()` + `VISITORS[]` + the `Cat` class)** â€” called first in `update()` each frame. `state.nextVisitor` holds the time of the next guest; once it passes (and no pellets are out), one maker is picked at random from the `VISITORS` registry and stored as `state.visitor` (one at a time). A visitor is any object with `update(dt)`, a `done` flag and an optional `spooks` flag; it's depth-sorted with the ducks in `drawScene` (item kind `4`, dispatched by `drawVisitor`). When the visitor `spooks`, the flock is woken and forced into a `huddle` state (scatter/regroup, see the cat). When the visitor `befriends` instead, `state.friend` is set and any duck the guest comes within ~130px of rolls (~72%) to enter the `visit` state â€” it ambles to a slot in a loose ring around the friend and emits the odd heart; un-joined ducks are marked `friendDecided` so they don't keep re-rolling. Both reactions clear on `done`/visitor-swap (ducks `pickIdle`, flags reset). On `done`, everything is cleared, the ducks `pickIdle()`, and the next visit is scheduled. **The `Cat`** is a self-contained state machine (`enter â†’ stalk â†’ pounce â†’ recover â†’ â€¦ â†’ leave`) that steers like a duck and crouches/lunges/hops/tail-swishes through `drawCat()`. He enters and leaves over the grass (`aim()` with no `water` option clamps to land via `landX()`), but when stalking/pouncing/chasing he follows the ducks onto the water, where a single `maxSpeed*=0.85` makes him 85% of his land speed (`drawCat` paints a waterline across his belly). Full state machine: `enter â†’ stalk â†’ pounce â†’ recover â†’ chase â†’ (stalk | leave)`. As he commits to a pounce and closes within ~58px, `spookFlock()` fires once â€” every duck gets a `panicT`/wide `panicAng` and explodes off in its own direction (dramatic, all-directions). After a brief `recover`, `nearestDuck()` is stored as `state.chased` and put into the duck `flee` state (sprints away at `RUN*1.02`, faster than the cat's `chase` speed of `84`); the cat chases it for `chaseDur` seconds, then gives up and re-stalks. `flockCentroid()` excludes the chased/fleeing duck so the rest regroup around their own centre, and the calm-huddle uses a distance-aware speed so they hustle back briskly while the cat is busy. He meows on a random timer via `audio.meow()`. `VISITORS` is now an array of `{name, make}` objects: the random rotation picks `.make()`, and the shop (`buildShop()` in the UI section) renders one row per entry from `.name`. `summonVisitor(maker)` is what the shop's Call buttons invoke â€” it clears any current guest (resetting `alarm`/`huddle`/`scare` and un-huddling the ducks) then spawns the chosen one and pushes back the auto-timer. To add an animal: write its class (with `update`/`draw`/`done`, plus a reaction flag â€” `spooks` (scare the flock), `befriends` (draw them in), `lures` (one duck gives futile chase), `curious` (one duck inspects it and gets startled), `mimic` (ducks line up and copy it), or none for a fully ignored guest like the koi), add a `drawX` case to `drawVisitor`, and push a `{name, make}` entry into `VISITORS` â€” it then appears in both the rotation and the shop automatically. The **`RedPanda`** is the worked example of a friendly land guest: `enter â†’ amble â†’ pause â†’ â€¦ â†’ leave`, staying east of `shoreX()` via a per-frame clamp, drawn by `drawPanda()` (rust body, dark legs/belly, bushy ringed tail, white masked face). The **`Stork`** is the worked example of a flying, neutral guest: `flyIn â†’ land â†’ peck â†’ flyOut`. It picks a `landX/landY` on the shoreline, glides down from above the screen (`y` starts at `-50`), settles, then runs a peck cycle (`headDip` 0â†’1â†’0 via `peckPhase`) that drops `spawnRipple` at the water mid-dip, and finally flies off the top. `drawStork()` switches between a flying pose (wings spread, neck forward, legs trailing) and a standing/pecking pose (long legs, S-neck, long beak that rotates down with `headDip`) based on its state. It also carries the sixth reaction flag, `mimic`: while the stork is in `peck`, `manageVisitor` recruits the nearest idle duck (within ~170px) roughly every couple of seconds into the duck `mimic` state â€” each gets a `mimicSlot` placing it at a spot along the shoreline beside the stork, and a `mimicPeck` timer that dips its head (reusing `headDip`) once it's settled. When the stork enters `flyOut`/`done` they all `pickIdle`. The **`Dragonfly`** is a swarm with a fourth reaction type, `lures`: each of its 2 `flies` darts between `overWaterPoint()`s (clamped west of `shoreX()`), and `manageVisitor`'s `lures` branch lets a single idle duck enter the `chaseFly` state to chase one fly (`state.dfChaser`/`dfTarget`). The chased fly flees at high speed so the duck can't win, and `chaseT` makes the duck give up. `drawDragonfly()` loops `drawDfly()` (long two-tone abdomen, big-eyed head, 4 translucent flapping wings, rotated to heading). The **`FrogFamily`** is a neutral guest showing follow-the-leader movement: `updateFrog()` runs a discrete hop (lerp along an arc with `z` height + `squash`/stretch + a landing splat); the `parent` roams between waterside `pickTarget()`s while each baby chases the frog ahead of it, only hopping when it falls more than ~30px behind (keeping a tidy line). After `life` they hop into the water and `done` fires with `spawnRipple` plops. `drawFrogFamily()` y-sorts and draws each via `drawFrog()` (green body, big eyes on top, smile, a back haunch/leg that extends mid-hop; babies are `size` 0.5). It also carries the fifth reaction flag, `curious`: `manageVisitor`'s `curious` branch sends one idle duck into the `curious` state (waddle toward `parent`); within ~40px it calls `FrogFamily.startle()` (a big leap away) and flips the duck to `frogShy` (scurry away ~1s, `shyT`) before it returns to `curious` â€” repeating until `curiosityT` runs out. The **`Koi`** is a school: the visitor holds `this.fish` (3 `makeKoiFish()` sprites), and `Koi.update` runs `updateKoiFish()` on each (one is the calm, non-spooking, water-bound example â€” wanders between random `waterPoint()`s, clamps west of `shoreX()`, runs a `surface` 0â†’1â†’0 cycle that spawns ripples + `audio.bloop()`). `drawKoi()` loops the school calling `drawKoiFish()`, which draws each fish top-down, rotated to its heading, opacity/teal-tint driven by `surface`. The school's `y` (avg of its fish) is used for depth sorting.
- **Sleepy idle (`manageSleep()`)** â€” called near the top of `update()` each frame. Tracks `state.lastFeed` (set in `dropPellet`); once `state.time - lastFeed` passes `SLEEP_DELAY` (and there are no pellets and no duck mid-meal), it picks a roost point at the flock's centroid and sends every duck to a golden-angle slot around it via a new `toRoost` state, after which each settles into a `sleep` state. Any new pellet flips `state.sleeping` off and calls `startWake()` on each duck (a brief `wake` stretch) before normal `assignFood()` seeking resumes. Sleeping/roosting/waking ducks are skipped by `assignFood()`. Each duck carries a `drowsy` 0â†’1 value (ramps up slowly, down briskly) that drives the head-tuck, closed eyes and breathing bob in `draw()`; `spawnZzz()` emits floating "z" particles while asleep.
- **Weather rendering** â€” `drawStarsAndMoon(g)` (twinkling stars from a once-generated `stars[]` map + a layered glowing moon) and `drawRain(g)` (falling streaks). `render()` applies a dark blue night wash behind the ducks and a lighter wash in front so ducks blend in and dim. Splash ripples are spawned in `update()` only over water (`isWater`) while it's raining.
- **Per-frame draw** â€” `render()` blits the static `bg`, then draws animated layers (clouds, water shimmer, ripples, depth-sorted ducks/pellets/lilies/reeds, particles, weather washes/effects, vignette).
- **`audio` module** â€” a self-contained IIFE using the Web Audio API. All sound is synthesised live (no audio files): a breeze bed, an airy shimmer, occasional birdsong, plus one-shot sounds (pellet plip, eat nom, happy chirp, a cat meow, and a koi "bloop"). All one-shots no-op unless sound is enabled.
- **Input / loop / resize** â€” pointer events for tap+drag feeding, the requestAnimationFrame loop, and canvas resize (re-runs scene build).
- **Service worker registration** â€” a small script at the very bottom.

Key technique: the duck body is drawn as an upright "bowling-pin" teardrop with a continuous tapered neck (a `ribbon()` helper builds the neck as one smooth shape) so it reads as an Indian Runner rather than a generic duck. Ducks swim when over the pond (body clipped at the waterline, legs hidden). Depth is faked by scaling ducks/objects by their y-position (`depthScale`).

## Tuning knobs (where to change common things)

All in `index.html`.

- **Ducks** â€” count is now driven by the shop (`state.duckCount`, default 1) and the shared record, not a constant. `setDuckCount(n)` adds/removes to match; the cap is `DUCK_LIMIT=100`. Prices: `duckCost()` (`500*count`) and `VISITOR_COST` (1500). To gift yourself currency for testing, set `state.currency` in the console or temporarily bump it.
- **Duck colours** â€” the `PALS` array. Add a 5th palette object to get a new colour into the rotation.
- **Duck speeds** â€” `RUN` and `WALK` near the top.
- **Food sharing** (how many ducks chase one pellet) â€” in `assignFood()`. The score uses `pe.claims * (0.1 * minS)` and a pellet is "full" at `claims >= 4`. Lower the multiplier / raise the cap = more ducks per pellet (tighter swarming); higher multiplier / lower cap = calmer, more spread out. (Tuned up from the original `0.6` penalty / cap of `2` to encourage group swarming.) Note the target lock-in was removed, so ducks re-pick a pellet each frame â€” no extra knob needed.
- **Weather location** â€” the lat/lon and API URL in `syncWeather()` (currently Wrexham, UK). Swap the coordinates to relocate the pond's weather. To make weather refresh during a session, wrap `syncWeather()` in a `setInterval` (it's a one-shot call today).
- **Rain intensity** â€” splash spawn rate is the `Math.random() < dt * 15` test in `update()`; streak look is in `drawRain()`.
- **Night look** â€” wash colours/opacity in `render()`'s night branches, plus the moon size/position and star count (`for(...i<80...)`) near the top.
- **Visitor frequency** â€” `var VISITOR_MIN=60, VISITOR_MAX=90;` near the top (seconds between guests = 1-1.5 min; drop these to, say, `8`/`15` to see visitors quickly while testing). Which animals can appear is the `VISITORS` array. Cat visit length is `this.life=rand(20,30)` in `Cat()`; its stalk/pounce timings, speeds and crouch are in `Cat.prototype.update`. How slow he is in water is the single `if(inWater) maxSpeed*=0.85` in `Cat.prototype.update`. The panic scatter is `spookFlock()`: `panicT=rand(1.3,2.4)` is how long they scarper, `panicAng=awayÂ±1.7` how widely they fan out, and the panic run is `RUN*1.0` over a 95px lead in the duck `huddle` branch. The chase: `chaseDur=rand(3.5,6.5)` (how long he pursues), `maxSpeed=84` in the cat `chase` state vs the fleeing duck's `RUN*1.02` (keep the duck faster), and the regroup briskness is the `clamp(d2h*1.6, WALK*1.3, RUN*0.85)` in the calm-huddle branch. The stalk now pounces on **proximity, not a timer**: he trots over (`maxSpeed=58`) while >150px from the flock, drops into a low creep (`maxSpeed=30`, crouch) once closer, and only pounces when within `this.pounceRange` (`rand(74,104)`, re-rolled each pounce) â€” so he lands near the ducks instead of on empty space (a 9s safety fallback pounces anyway if he somehow can't close in). The pounce distance that sets off the scatter is the `<58` test; how far he dives in is `stopAt:16`. Meow cadence is `this.meowT=rand(4.5,9.5)`.
- **Red panda** â€” visit length is `this.life=rand(28,44)` in `RedPanda()`; amble speed is `maxSpeed=34`. How many ducks befriend it is the `<130` range + `<0.72` roll in `manageVisitor`'s befriend branch (raise either for a bigger gathering); the ring they form is `vr=34+(this.huddleSlot%3)*11` in the duck `visit` branch, and heart frequency is the `dt*0.3` there.
- **Frogs** â€” number of babies is the `for(var i=0;i<3;i++)` in `FrogFamily()`; visit length `this.life=rand(20,34)`. Hop size/height is `step`/`f.hopH` in `updateFrog`; the line spacing is the `>30` / `d-22` gap there. Where they roam is `pickTarget()` (a band around `shoreX`). The curiosity is the `<140` range + `<dt*0.7` roll (a duck taking interest), the `<40` trigger distance, the startle leap height (`p.hopH=16` in `startle()`), and `curiosityT=rand(7,13)` (how long before the duck gives up).
- **Dragonflies** â€” count is the `this.flies=[makeDfly(), makeDfly()]` in `Dragonfly()`; visit length `this.life=rand(18,30)`. Dart speed is `maxSpeed=140` (wander) / `195` (fleeing a duck) in `updateDfly`; how easily a duck takes the bait is the `<64` range + `<dt*1.6` roll in the `lures` branch, and how long it chases is `chaseT=rand(2.5,5.5)`. Colours are the `col` ternary in `makeDfly()`. When chased, a fly uses **heading-based steering** (in `updateDfly`): it computes a desired direction (away from the duck + a wall-repulsion term that starts at `M=120`) and turns its actual `heading` toward it at a capped rate (`6.5*dt`), so it banks in smooth curves, can never spin at an edge, and arcs out to open water (it'll happily zoom past the duck). On `leaving` it rises off the top (`ty=-110`, with the `yLo` clamp letting it exit the screen so the swarm despawns).
- **Stork** â€” how long it fishes is `this.life=rand(13,22)` in `Stork()`; flight speed is the `maxSpeed=120/130` in `flyIn`/`flyOut`; peck cadence is `this.peckT=rand(1.1,2.6)` and each dip lasts `0.55`s (the `/0.55` in `peck`). Landing spot is `landX=shoreX(landY)-5` (right at the water's edge). The mimicry is the `mimic` branch in `manageVisitor`: recruit range (`<170` of the stork), rate (`<dt*0.5`), max line length (`mc<6`), and the line spacing (`*30` per slot in the duck `mimic` branch).
- **Koi** â€” school size is the `for(var i=0;i<3;i++)` in `Koi()`; visit length is `this.life=rand(24,38)`. Per-fish: glide speed `maxSpeed=20+...` and `dt*1.8` steer in `updateKoiFish`, surface frequency `nextSurf=rand(4.5,10)`, dwell `surfDur=rand(1.3,2.4)`. Colour variants (white-kohaku vs all-orange) are the `pal` ternary in `makeKoiFish()`.
- **Sleep timing** â€” `var SLEEP_MIN=60, SLEEP_MAX=120;` near the top (1-2 min of no feeding; a fresh value is rolled into `state.sleepDelay` by `rollSleepDelay()` on each feed). Huddle tightness is the `rad=20+(i%3)*13` slots in `manageSleep()`. Fall-asleep vs wake speed is the asymmetric `drowsyRate` (1.4 up / 4.5 down) in `Duck.update`. Zzz frequency is the `this.zzzT=2.2+...` reset in `Duck.update`; "z" look is the `type==='zzz'` branch in `drawParticles`.
- **Audio bed** â€” in `startAmbient()`:
  - Breeze volume: `windGain.gain.value = 0.03`
  - Breeze tone: `wbp.frequency.value = 700` (lower = deeper/softer)
  - Gust strength: `gustG.gain.value = 0.035`
  - Water shimmer: `shimGain.gain.value = 0.01` (set to 0 to remove)
- **Birds** â€” how often: the delay in `scheduleBird()` is `1500 + Math.random()*9000` ms. Pitch: `base = 1950 + Math.random()*1300` in `bird()`. Song variety is the four `type` branches in `bird()`.

## Currency & saving (groundwork for the shop)

- **Earning** â€” `addCurrency()` is called from `eatPellet()` (`+EARN_PER_PELLET`, currently 1, only when a duck eats) and once per frame in `update()` (`EARN_PER_MINUTE/60*dt`, currently +100/min while the tab is visible â€” it pauses when hidden because the loop stops). `state.currency` is a float; the UI shows `Math.floor`.
- **Display** â€” `#coin` pill (CSS near `#title`), text refreshed by `updateCoinUI()` at the end of `render()` (only writes the DOM when the floored value changes). Label comes from the `CURRENCY` constant.
- **Saving / sharing (Firebase)** â€” the `store` object owns all persistence. `store.load()` reads `localStorage` first (instant + offline), then `store._connect()` brings up Firebase: `firebase.initializeApp(FIREBASE_CONFIG)` â†’ `signInAnonymously()` (the DB rules require `auth != null`) â†’ subscribes to `ponds/shared/currency` with `ref.on('value')`. Earnings are accrued locally into `_pendingEarn` and committed on each autosave (`store.save`, every ~5s + on hide/pagehide) as an **atomic `ref.transaction(c => c + delta)`**, so two phones earning at once both count (no clobber). The `on('value')` callback makes the shared total the source of truth (`state.currency = remote + _pendingEarn`). `store.spend(n)` (for the future shop) is an atomic `c => max(0, c - n)`. The Firebase **compat** SDK is loaded as three `<script>` tags in `<head>` (so the global `firebase` is ready before the IIFE); if they fail to load (offline) the app falls back to per-device `localStorage`.
  - Firebase setup that lives outside the code: Realtime Database enabled, **Anonymous** sign-in enabled, and rules locking `ponds/shared` to `auth != null`. Config is in `FIREBASE_CONFIG` (public client config â€” safe in the client).
  - Known minor caveat: earnings accrued while fully offline live only in `localStorage` and are replaced by the shared total on reconnect (not folded in). Fine for normal online use; revisit if it matters.
- **Shop / shared world** â€” the shared record is now the whole `ponds/shared` object: `{ currency, ducks, unlocked:{id:1} }`. `store.load`â†’`_connect` subscribes to the whole node; `applyPond(p)` mirrors it into `state.currency` (+`_pendingEarn`), `setDuckCount(p.ducks)` (grows/shrinks the on-screen flock, leaving existing ducks be), and `state.unlocked`, then calls `refreshShopUI()`. Purchases are atomic transactions on the whole node that re-check affordability against the *shared* balance, so two phones buying at once can't overspend: `store.buyDuck()` (cost `500*ducks`, cap `DUCK_LIMIT`=100) and `store.buyVisitor(id)` (`VISITOR_COST`=1500, sets `unlocked[id]`). Earnings still commit via `store.save` (transaction `currency += pendingEarn`). Offline, all of these fall back to mutating `state` + `localStorage`. The random visitor scheduler only spawns from `unlockedVisitors()`; `buyVisitor` also summons the new guest once. `setDuckCount`/`applyPond` run on every remote update, so a purchase on one phone appears on the other.

## Known gotchas

- **iOS silent switch**: iOS mutes Web Audio when the physical ring/silent switch is on, regardless of volume. Mitigated in code by requesting the `'playback'` audio session (`navigator.audioSession.type`) and playing a silent unlock buffer inside the tap that enables sound. Net effect: once a user taps the speaker icon, sound plays even if the phone is set to silent.
- **Audio pauses on lock/background**: the `'playback'` session above also makes iOS keep audio running when the screen locks, which we don't want. Fixed with `audio.setActive(false/true)` â€” it suspends the `AudioContext` (and clears the bird timer) on `visibilitychange`â†’hidden / `pagehide` / `blur`, and resumes on return. The on/off state (`enabled`) is untouched, so the speaker icon stays "on" and sound comes back when the app is reopened. (Best confirmed on a real iPhone; on desktop it triggers on tab-switch.)
- **Caching**: see the update workflow above â€” one refresh after deploy; bump cache name to force-reset.
- **Service worker scope**: `sw.js` must stay at the repo root (its scope is its own directory).
- **Audio needs a user gesture**: the AudioContext is created/resumed on the first tap of the speaker button (browser requirement). Don't try to start sound on page load.
- **Weather is fetched once, on load**: `syncWeather()` is only called at startup, so a session that spans dusk won't flip to night until reloaded. Open-Meteo is a free, key-less endpoint; if the request fails (offline, blocked, rate-limited) the app silently keeps daytime/no-rain defaults. The CSP / service-worker caching doesn't intercept the API call (it's cross-origin, network-only).
- **Tap highlight**: `-webkit-tap-highlight-color: transparent;` in the global CSS removes the blue flash from rapid tapping on mobile â€” keep it if you touch the CSS reset.
